Cohort Zero:
Vibe Coding Bootcamp

A private, invite-only immersion for senior professionals.

Venue TBD, San Francisco, CA.

Most people trying to “learn AI” are doing it alone, stuck in online courses they’ll never finish, distracted on noisy Discords, or overwhelmed by multiple accounts across various tools without guidance.

What they’re missing isn’t content. It’s context: a focused environment, real-time feedback, and the energy of building alongside others like them. This is what AI can’t replicate: a live, in-person cohort experience.

No lectures, no fluff. Just learning by sitting in a room with others who are motivated like you to get better at prompting, using coding platforms to ship real projects, getting stuck and unstuck fast.

Each cohort becomes:

1. A Private Alumni Network

This isn't networking in the transactional sense. It's something rarer: a shared starting point. Everyone in the room is admitting they don't know this yet. That vulnerability creates trust. And that trust becomes the foundation for real collaboration down the road.

2. A Recruiting Pipeline

What AI-native companies actually care about is demonstrated fluency. Can you actually build something? Can you prompt your way through a problem? Do you know when to trust the output and when to push back?

We look for people who've put in the reps. Not people who watched a webinar, but people who've sat in the discomfort of learning something new and come out the other side with real capability.

3. Frontline Intelligence

Direct insight into the tech stacks, internal pain points, and automation wishlists of top-tier companies. It's the unfiltered reality of what's actually happening inside companies right now:

  • What tools are collecting dust?
  • Where are the bottlenecks in every sprint?
  • What manual processes are driving people crazy?

The Goal

Equip professionals with AI fluency to become internal champions who can effectively integrate AI tools and practices within their organizations.

Why Now?

AI fluency is no longer optional, it’s the new baseline for modern work. We’re in a critical window where the tools are powerful but still emerging. The next 12–18 months will define who leads in this new era, and who gets left behind.
